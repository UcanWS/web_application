Ability, Obligation, Permission & Possibility

Ability

Expressing what someone can do or is capable of doing.

Present:

  • can — I can swim very well.
  • be able to — She is able to solve complex problems.

Past:

  • could — He could run fast when he was younger.
  • was/were able to — They were able to escape the fire safely.

Future:

  • will be able to — You will be able to drive next year.

Obligation

Talking about what is necessary or required.

Present:

  • must / have to — You must wear a helmet. / I have to work late today.

Past:

  • had to — We had to leave early last night.

Future:

  • will have to — He will have to study harder for the exam.

Permission

Talking about what is allowed.

Present:

  • can / may — Can I use your phone? / You may enter the room now.

Past:

  • could / was allowed to — When I was a kid, I could stay up late. / He was allowed to leave early.

Future:

  • will be allowed to — You will be allowed to travel once you get your visa.

Possibility

Talking about what might happen or be true.

Present/Future:

  • may / might / could — It may rain later. / She might be at home now.

Past:

  • might have / could have — They might have missed the bus. / He could have forgotten the time.

Other forms:

  • be likely to — He is likely to win the competition.
  • be possible / impossible — It is possible that they’ll arrive late.
