Narrative Forms & Time Linkers

Narrative Forms

Narrative forms are verb tenses and structures used to tell stories or describe past events. The most common ones include:

  • Past Simple — used for completed actions in the past.
    Example: She went to the market yesterday.
  • Past Continuous — used for ongoing actions in the past or background situations.
    Example: I was walking when it started to rain.
  • Past Perfect — used to show one action happened before another in the past.
    Example: He had left before I arrived.
  • Past Perfect Continuous — emphasizes duration before something in the past.
    Example: They had been waiting for hours.

Tips for narrative writing:

  • Use past tenses consistently.
  • Vary sentence length for effect.
  • Include time linkers and vivid verbs.

Time Linkers

Time linkers are words or phrases used to show the order of events or to connect actions in time. They make stories clearer and more organized.

Examples & Usage:

  • First / Firstly — to begin a sequence.
    Firstly, we visited the museum.
  • Then / Next / After that — to show what happened later.
    Next, we had lunch at a small café.
  • Meanwhile — to describe something happening at the same time.
    Meanwhile, John was waiting outside.
  • When / While — to show simultaneous or triggering events.
    While I was walking, I saw a lost dog.
  • After / Before — to sequence actions.
    After we finished dinner, we went for a walk.
  • Eventually / Finally — to show the last event.
    Finally, we returned home tired but happy.

Tip: Use a variety of linkers to keep your writing smooth and engaging.
