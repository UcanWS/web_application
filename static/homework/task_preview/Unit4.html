Will vs Be Going To (Predictions & Decisions)

Will vs Be Going To

In English, we often use will and be going to to talk about the future. They are similar, but used in different situations. Here's how to know which one to choose:

1. Will

Form: Subject + will + base verb

Use "will" for:

  • Decisions made at the moment of speaking
  • "I’m tired." → "I will help you."
  • Promises or offers
  • I will call you tomorrow.
  • Predictions based on what we think, believe, or guess
  • I think it will snow tonight.

2. Be Going To

Form: Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb

Use "going to" for:

  • Plans or intentions made before now
  • We are going to visit grandma this weekend.
  • Predictions based on what we see or know now
  • Look at those dark clouds! It is going to rain.

3. Quick Comparison

Use Will Be going to
Prediction (guess, belief) It will be cold tomorrow. –
Prediction (based on now) – Look! He is going to fall.
Decision at the moment I will answer the phone. –
Planned action – I am going to meet her later.

Tip: Use will when you decide **now**, and going to when you already planned it or see it happening.

4. Talking about Probability: Will / May / Might

We often use will, may, and might to talk about how likely something is in the future.

  • Will = 100% sure or very certain
  • She will pass the exam. (I’m sure)
  • May = 50–70% chance
  • He may come to the party. (possibly yes)
  • Might = 30–50% chance (less certain than "may")
  • We might go to the beach. (not sure)

Grammar: All three use the base verb.

Subject + will/may/might + base verb

Examples:

  • I will visit grandma tomorrow. (certain)
  • I may visit grandma tomorrow. (possible)
  • I might visit grandma tomorrow. (less likely)

Note: Don’t use "will/may/might" with “to” or "-ing": ❌ He will to go / She may going
