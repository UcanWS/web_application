Present Simple

Form: Subject + base verb (-s/-es for he/she/it)

Usage:

  • Regular actions or habits: I walk to work every day.
  • General truths or facts: Water boils at 100°C.
  • Permanent situations: She lives in London.
  • Scheduled events (timetables): The train leaves at 6 pm.

Examples:

  • They study English every Monday.
  • He doesn't like spicy food.
  • Do you play tennis?

Present Continuous

Form: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

Usage:

  • Actions happening right now: She is reading a book.
  • Temporary situations: I am staying at my friend’s place this week.
  • Future arrangements (plans): We are meeting Sarah tomorrow.
  • Trends or changing situations: The climate is getting warmer.

Examples:

  • They aren't watching TV now.
  • Are you coming to the party?
  • He is always arriving late. (complaint)

Present Perfect Simple

Form: Subject + have/has + past participle

Usage:

  • Actions at an unspecified time before now: I have visited Paris.
  • Life experiences: She has never eaten sushi.
  • Actions repeated in an unspecified period: We have seen that movie three times.
  • Actions that started in the past and continue to now (with for/since): He has worked here since 2010.

Examples:

  • They have just arrived.
  • I haven’t finished my homework yet.
  • Have you ever been to New York?

State Verbs

Definition: Verbs that describe a state, condition, emotion, possession, sense, or thought, rather than an action. They are usually not used in continuous (-ing) forms.

Common categories & examples:

  • Emotions/feelings: love, hate, like, dislike, prefer
    — I love chocolate. (not am loving)
  • Possession/ownership: have, own, belong, possess
    — She has two cars. (not is having)
  • Senses: see, hear, taste, smell, feel
    — This soup tastes great.
  • Thoughts/opinions: know, believe, think, understand, mean
    — I believe in you. (not am believing)
  • Measurements/quantity: weigh, cost, measure, contain
    — The box weighs 5 kg.

Note: Some verbs can be both state and action verbs with different meanings. E.g. think (opinion) vs. thinking (considering):
— I think you’re right. (opinion)
— I’m thinking about your offer. (process)
