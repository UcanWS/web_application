Unit 7.1: Used to and Would for Past Habits and States

Used to & Would – Talking About the Past

1. Used to

Form: used to + base verb

Usage: Describes past habits or states that are no longer true.

Examples:

  • I used to live in Spain. (but now I don’t)
  • She used to have long hair. (now she doesn’t)
  • We didn’t use to like sushi, but now we do.
  • Did you use to go to that school?

Notes:

  • We use “did/didn’t” in questions/negatives: Did you use to play football?
  • Not: ❌ Did you used to...

2. Would

Form: would + base verb

Usage: Describes repeated past actions (habits), NOT states.

Examples:

  • Every summer, we would go to the beach.
  • My dad would tell us stories before bed.

⚠️ Don’t use "would" for past states:

  • ❌ I would be shy. ✅ I used to be shy.
  • ❌ He would have a car. ✅ He used to have a car.

Compare:

  • When I was a child, I used to love cartoons. ✅ (state)
  • Every Saturday, we would ride our bikes for hours. ✅ (habit)
Unit 7.2: Questions – Object, Subject & Indirect

1. Subject Questions

These ask *who* or *what* is doing the action. The word order is like a normal sentence.

  • Who called you? (subject = who)
  • What happened next?

2. Object Questions

These ask about the object of the verb. They use auxiliary verbs like do/does/did.

  • Who did you call?
  • What did she break?

3. Indirect Questions

These are more polite or formal. Use them in reported speech or to sound more polite.

  • Direct: Where is the station?
    Indirect: Could you tell me where the station is?
  • Direct: What time does the train leave?
    Indirect: Do you know what time the train leaves?

Note: In indirect questions, we don’t use do/does/did and the word order changes.

Unit 7.3: Understanding Paraphrasing

1. Using synonyms

Replace words with similar meanings.

  • Original: He helped me a lot.
    Paraphrase: He assisted me greatly.

2. Changing grammar structures

  • Active to passive:
    Original: They built the house.
    Paraphrase: The house was built by them.
  • Changing clause types:
    Original: Although it was late, she stayed.
    Paraphrase: Despite being late, she stayed.

3. Simplifying or combining ideas

  • Original: He was tired. He went to bed early.
    Paraphrase: Because he was tired, he went to bed early.

Why paraphrase? To show understanding, vary your language, avoid repetition, and improve clarity.
