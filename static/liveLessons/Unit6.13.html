Extreme Adjectives and Linkers

Extreme Adjectives

What are they? Extreme adjectives are stronger versions of normal adjectives.

Examples:

  • tired → exhausted
  • cold → freezing
  • hungry → starving
  • good → fantastic / amazing / great

We usually don’t use "very" with extreme adjectives:

  • ❌ I’m very exhausted
  • ✅ I’m exhausted

We can use: absolutely, really, completely

  • ✅ I’m absolutely freezing.
  • ✅ That meal was really delicious.

Linkers

What are linkers? Linkers are words that connect ideas, sentences, or paragraphs.

Types of linkers

  • Adding information: and, also, in addition, moreover
  • Contrasting: but, however, although, whereas
  • Cause and effect: because, so, therefore, as a result

Examples:

  • She was tired, but she kept working.
  • It rained all day. As a result, the game was cancelled.
Unit 6.2: Present Perfect Simple and Continuous

Present Perfect Simple vs. Continuous

Present Perfect Simple

Form: have/has + past participle

Usage:

  • Finished actions with results: I’ve lost my keys.
  • Life experience: She has visited Japan.
  • Repeated actions: We’ve been there several times.

Present Perfect Continuous

Form: have/has + been + verb-ing

Usage:

  • Actions continuing up to now: I’ve been studying for 2 hours.
  • Recent actions (you can see the result): She’s been crying.

Compare:

  • I’ve read five books this month. (result)
  • I’ve been reading for 2 hours. (activity)
Unit 6.3: Adjectives and Suffixes

Adjectives and Suffixes

We can make adjectives by adding suffixes to nouns or verbs.

Common Suffixes:

  • -ful: hope → hopeful, care → careful
  • -less: help → helpless, use → useless
  • -ous: danger → dangerous, fame → famous
  • -able/-ible: enjoy → enjoyable, access → accessible
  • -ive: act → active, create → creative

Examples:

  • He gave a hopeful speech.
  • That movie was completely useless.
  • We need a more creative solution.
